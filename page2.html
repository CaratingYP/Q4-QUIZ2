CHARACTERTS IN NOLI ME TANGERE


Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magasalin A wealthy young man of mixed Spanish and Filipino descent who has just returned to the Philippines from Europe after studying abroad for seven years Ibarra is well-educated and respected, yet the friars in his homeland of San Diego are uneasy about him. He has a lover named Maria Clara.
Maria Clara de los Santos y Alba She became the epitome of virtue, "demure and self-effacing," and blessed with beauty, grace, and charm as a devout Roman Catholic. She is an "ideal image" of a Filipino woman worthy of being set on the "pedestal of male honor". She has a lover named Crisostomo Ibarra.
Padre Bernardo Salvi Padre Salvi is a Franciscan friar from San Diego. His physical appearance is thin, pale, and he always appears unwell. People in his town praised him, and he does not abuse people, but he makes them pay for their mistakes. He raped Maria Clara at the end of the story.
Padre Damaso Verdolagas He is an extrovert who is arrogant, cruel, and judgmental. He has no control over his remarks and does not care if the person he is speaking to feels ashamed or guilty. He is always berating or criticizing those around him, particularly Ibarra. He is the godfather and Maria Clara's biological father.
